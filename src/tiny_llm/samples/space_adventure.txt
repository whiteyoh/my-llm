Mina and Jo packed a tiny lunch and stepped into their bright blue rocket.
They counted down from ten and launched toward the moon.
At each window, they wrote notes about stars, shadows, and silent craters.
When they landed, a friendly rover blinked and said, "Welcome, explorers!"
They planted a small flag, collected smooth rocks, and flew safely home.

Captain Rowan checked the star map while the station lights glowed softly.
The crew moved quietly through the silver hallway and listened to the engine hum.
Mira carried a notebook full of moon sketches, comet trails, and careful questions.
Every time a new planet appeared, the class wrote down its color, shape, and orbit.
The ship did not rush. It drifted gently so everyone could observe the sky.

At sunrise on Mars, the rover rolled past red stones and dusty hills.
Jo measured the shadow of a solar panel and compared it with yesterday's notes.
Mina found a pebble shaped like a teardrop and placed it in a sample box.
Captain Rowan reminded the crew that good explorers look closely before they guess.
The radio crackled with a message from home: "Tell us what you notice first."

By evening, the rocket pointed toward a ringed planet.
The rings looked like chalk lines across a dark classroom board.
Mira whispered that space felt quiet, but the instruments were always speaking.
The crew watched numbers change on the screen and learned to trust evidence.
When the lesson ended, the rocket turned home under a sky full of patient stars.
