Robo-Bean is a classroom helper with wheels, lights, and polite manners.
Each morning, it greets students and reminds them of the reading schedule.
It can carry books, sort pencils, and play calm music during quiet work time.
When someone asks for help, Robo-Bean says, "Let's solve it step by step."

On Monday, Robo-Bean rolled to the science table and counted six magnets.
It placed red labels on the north poles and blue labels on the south poles.
Then it projected a gentle timer so each group had the same time to observe.
"Notice first, explain second," Robo-Bean said in its soft classroom voice.

On Tuesday, the robot helped with a story circle.
It listened for kind turns, raised a tiny light when the next reader was ready,
and saved the class questions on a tablet for the teacher.
When a student felt stuck, Robo-Bean suggested, "Try one clue, then another."

On Wednesday, Robo-Bean sorted art supplies by color, size, and texture.
It did not rush the work. It checked each tray twice and asked for human review.
The class laughed when it placed glitter beside the stars and called them cousins.
Robo-Bean blinked green and wrote, "Creative categories can be useful."

On Friday, the class thanked Robo-Bean for helping everyone stay organized.
The robot bowed, spun once, and printed a tiny checklist for next week.
It reminded the class that tools are helpful when people choose thoughtful goals.
Then Robo-Bean parked by the bookshelf and charged quietly for Monday.
