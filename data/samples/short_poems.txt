Rain taps softly
on the old classroom window.
Robots dream in chalk.

Small moon above,
a satellite hums to sleep
in a paper sky.

Garden bell ringing,
butterflies lift from lavender.
Pencils pause to watch.

Silver hallway lights
glow along the quiet station.
Stars lean close to hear.

Rover wheels whisper
over dust and red stone.
Footprints fill with sun.

A kettle sings low
near the mast at morning tide.
Maps curl in the breeze.

Soft green diode blink,
Robo-Bean sorts blue pencils.
Classmates count along.

Cloud shadows drift by.
The pond keeps every color
until wind arrives.

Comet trail fading,
Mira labels one more note:
signals, light, and home.

After the lesson,
chairs tuck under wooden desks.
Questions stay awake.
