The station woke as the comet passed.
Mira pressed her hand to the glass and watched cities of light bloom across the hull.
Every window showed a different reflection: stars, cables, notebooks, sleepy faces.
The morning bell sounded like a small silver chime.

In the greenhouse ring, rows of basil turned toward artificial sunrise.
Kai checked the water sensors and wrote the numbers on a slate.
The plants did not know they were orbiting a cold moon, but they leaned toward light.
Mira liked that lesson: living things answer the signals they receive.

At noon, a message arrived from the far relay.
It was not a mystery, only a weather report from another station.
Still, the class gathered close as the screen filled with dots and lines.
They decoded pressure, temperature, and dust patterns one careful symbol at a time.

By evening, the comet trail faded behind the station.
The hull lights dimmed to a gentle blue.
Mira saved the day's notes under the title "Signals and Shadows."
Then the station turned quietly, carrying its classroom through the dark.
